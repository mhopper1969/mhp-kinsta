Martyn Hopper

Founder & Managing Director — Law, regulation and public policy

Overview

Martyn is a practising solicitor and the founder of Martyn Hopper & Partners. His work brings together legal, regulatory and policy expertise developed over three decades in the UK’s financial services sector — including almost ten years at the Financial Services Authority and twenty-one years as a partner at leading City law firms Herbert Smith and Linklaters.

He advises Boards and senior executives on regulatory exposure, governance and accountability, and the design of systems and cultures that enable sustainable growth. His approach combines regulatory insight, strategic clarity and operational realism — helping clients navigate complex, high-stakes environments with confidence.

Focus

  • Advising on senior management accountability, conduct and culture frameworks.
  • Complex investigations, enforcement and skilled-person reviews.
  • Regulatory risk assessments and programme design for Consumer Duty, governance and control functions.
  • Board and executive coaching on regulatory decision-making and communications.
  • Engagement with regulators, investors and other stakeholders in periods of scrutiny or change.

Career

  • Partner, Herbert Smith LLP — Financial Services Regulation.
  • Partner, Linklaters LLP — Financial Regulation and Enforcement.
  • Head of Enforcement Policy and Head of Market Abuse, Financial Services Authority (FSA).

Approach

Martyn’s work is grounded in a belief that regulation should promote clarity, accountability and trust. He helps clients build frameworks that withstand regulatory challenge while supporting commercial ambition — enabling firms to operate with both confidence and conscience.
